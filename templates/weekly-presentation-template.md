# Weekly PowerPoint Presentation Template

**AI Practitioner Training Curriculum**

---

## Template Overview

This template provides a standardized structure for creating weekly session PowerPoint presentations for the AI Practitioner Training program. Each weekly presentation follows a consistent format designed for 45-minute live sessions delivered via MS Teams.

---

## Presentation Metadata

```markdown
# **POWERPOINT PRESENTATION: [BLOCK X] WEEK [Y]**
## **[WEEK TITLE]**

**Block:** [1: AI Prompting Mastery | 2: AI Workflow Engineering | 3: AI Automation Architecture]
**Week Number:** [1-8]
**Session Duration:** 45 minutes
**Delivery Format:** Live MS Teams workshop

**Target Audience:** [Description - e.g., "Consultants with Block 1 certification"]

**Week Learning Objectives:** By the end of this session, participants will:
1. [Objective 1 - specific, measurable skill or knowledge]
2. [Objective 2]
3. [Objective 3]

**Entry Criteria:** (What participants should have before this session)
- [ ] [Prerequisite 1]
- [ ] [Prerequisite 2]
- [ ] [Prerequisite 3]

**Exit Criteria:** (What participants should be able to do after this session)
- [ ] [Skill/Knowledge 1]
- [ ] [Skill/Knowledge 2]
- [ ] [Skill/Knowledge 3]
- [ ] [Skill/Knowledge 4]

**Presentation Structure:**
1. Opening & Recap (3 min) - Slides 1-3
2. Segment 1: [Topic] (X min) - Slides 4-Y
3. Segment 2: [Topic] (X min) - Slides Y-Z
4. Segment 3: [Topic] (X min) - Slides Z-A
5. Segment 4: [Topic] (X min) - Slides A-B
6. Homework Preview & Close (3 min) - Slides B-End

**Total Slides:** [Number]
```

## Slide Definitions

### SLIDE 1: TITLE SLIDE

**Title:** [Block X] Week [Y]: [Week Title]

**Subtitle:** [Descriptive subtitle that captures the week's theme]

**Content:**
- [Instructor Name]
- [Date/Cohort identifier]
- AI Practitioner Training Program

**Graphic:** Clean title slide with program branding. Include block color coding:
- Block 1: Blue tones (foundations/learning)
- Block 2: Orange tones (building/engineering)
- Block 3: Green tones (automation/architecture)

**SPEAKER NOTES:**

"[OPENING - Welcome]"

"Welcome to Week [Y] of [Block name]. Today we're focusing on [brief topic overview].

[If Week 1 of block]: This is our first session of [Block name], so we'll start with an overview of what this block is about and what you'll achieve over the next 8 weeks.

[If Week 2-7]: Last week we covered [previous topic]. Who can share one thing they applied from last week's homework?

[Wait for 1-2 responses]

Great. Today we're building on that foundation with [this week's focus]."

[Transition: Click to next slide]

### SLIDE 4: SEGMENT 1 - PROBLEM/HOOK

**Title:** [Attention-grabbing title - question or problem statement]

**Content:**

**The Challenge:**
[Describe the problem this segment addresses - make it relatable to consulting work]

**Why It Matters:**
- [Impact 1 - connect to their daily work]
- [Impact 2 - connect to client value]
- [Impact 3 - connect to career development]

**Graphic:** Visual that illustrates the problem or pain point. Could be:
- Before/after comparison
- Chart showing impact
- Illustration of the frustration
- Quote from practitioner about the challenge

**SPEAKER NOTES:**

"[Hook - Create tension]"

"Let me ask you a question: [rhetorical question related to the problem]

[Pause - let question land]

[Personal story or common scenario - adjust to your context]

The reality is [state the problem clearly]. And this costs us [specific impact - time, quality, money, frustration].

[Point to graphic]

[Explain what the visual shows]

Today we're going to solve this. Here's how..."

[Transition]

**BACKGROUND:**

**Rationale:**
- This slide establishes the "why" before the "how"
- Creates emotional buy-in for the solution that follows
- Connects abstract concepts to real consulting pain points
- Sets up the value proposition for the technique being taught

**Q&A Preparation:**
- *"Is this really that big of a problem?"*: Yes - [provide data or example showing impact]
- *"I've never experienced this issue"*: You may not have noticed it yet, but [explain how it manifests]

**Key Research & Citations:**

- **[Framework Reference]**: [Established methodology this builds on]

**Implementation Guidance:**

**Getting Started:**

**Common Pitfalls:**

### SLIDE 6: SEGMENT 1 - LIVE DEMO

**Title:** Live Demo: [What you're demonstrating]

**Content:**

**Demo Scenario:**
[Brief description of what will be demonstrated]

**Watch For:**
- [Key point to notice #1]
- [Key point to notice #2]
- [Key point to notice #3]

**Graphic:** Screenshot placeholder or visual showing the demo setup

**SPEAKER NOTES:**

"[DEMO - Show, don't tell]"

"Let me show you exactly how this works.

[Narrate what you're doing as you do it]

[Step 1]: Notice how I'm [action] - this is important because [reason].

[Step 2]: Now I'm going to [action]. Watch what happens...

[Point to result]: See how [observation]? This is the [technique name] in action.

[If demo includes before/after]: Compare this to what we saw before. The difference is [specific observation].

[Common mistake to avoid]: If you do [wrong approach], you'll get [bad result]. That's why we [correct approach].

[Pause]

Questions about what you just saw?"

**BACKGROUND:**

**Demo Backup Plan:**
If live demo fails:
1. Use prepared screenshots showing each step
2. Walk through the process conceptually
3. Offer to demonstrate 1:1 in office hours

**Timing Note:**
- Demo should take 5-8 minutes maximum
- Have stopping point identified if running long
- Better to show one complete example than rush multiple

## Appendix: Slide Type Reference

Use these slide types as building blocks:

| Slide Type | Purpose | When to Use |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| **Title** | Opens presentation | Slide 1 only |
| **Overview/Agenda** | Sets expectations | After title |
| **Learning Objectives** | Frames outcomes | Before content begins |
| **Problem/Hook** | Creates tension | Opens each segment |
| **Concept Introduction** | Introduces new idea | After establishing problem |
| **Framework/Model** | Shows structure | For methodologies, patterns |
| **Demo** | Shows technique in action | After concept explanation |
| **Comparison** | Contrasts approaches | Before/after, good/bad |
| **Example** | Concrete illustration | After abstract concepts |
| **Key Takeaway** | Summarizes segment | Closes each segment |
| **Homework Preview** | Sets up exercises | Closing section |
| **Resources** | Provides support links | Closing section |
| **Next Week Preview** | Creates continuity | Final slide |

## Appendix: Speaker Notes Conventions

### Stage Directions (in brackets)
- `[Pause]` - Brief silence for effect
- `[Pause for effect]` - Longer pause after key statement
- `[Point to X]` - Gesture to visual element
- `[Show of hands]` - Audience participation
- `[Wait for responses]` - After asking real question
- `[Demo starts]` - Beginning live demonstration
- `[Personal story - adjust to your context]` - Customize with your experience

### Section Markers
- `[OPENING - description]` - Session opening
- `[Hook - Create tension]` - Attention grabber
- `[INSIGHT - Deliver solution]` - Key revelation
- `[DEMO - Show, don't tell]` - Demonstration section
- `[Transition]` - Moving to next slide
- `[Transition: Click to Segment X]` - Major section change

### Timing Notes
- Include "[X min]" markers at key points
- Note "[If running short...]" alternatives
- Mark "[Can skip if needed]" for optional content

## Appendix: Visual Design Guidelines

### Slide Layout Principles
- Maximum 6 bullet points per slide
- Maximum 6-8 words per bullet
- One main idea per slide
- Consistent header placement
- Generous white space

### Block Color Coding
| Block | Primary Color | Accent Color | Meaning |
|-------|--------------|--------------|---------|
| Block 1 | Blue (#0066CC) | Light Blue | Foundations, Learning |
| Block 2 | Orange (#FF6B35) | Amber | Building, Engineering |
| Block 3 | Green (#00CC99) | Teal | Automation, Architecture |

### Font Hierarchy
- Slide Title: 32-36pt, Bold
- Section Header: 24-28pt, Bold
- Body Text: 18-20pt, Regular
- Captions/Notes: 14-16pt, Italic

### Graphic Types
- **Flow diagrams** - For processes and sequences
- **Framework illustrations** - For models and structures
- **Before/after comparisons** - For demonstrating impact
- **Screenshots** - For software demonstrations
- **Tables** - For comparisons and reference data
- **Icons** - For quick visual cues
